Publish your next series

Choose the platform, plan your release cadence, and package your story for readers across webtoon, serial fiction, and print marketplaces. Here’s a quick map of the most important publishing options.

Webtoon-ready formatting Multi-platform metadata Monetization strategy Rights & exclusivity

Webtoon Originals

Best for vertical scroll series with weekly releases and editorial collaboration.

  • Target: 40-60 panels per episode.
  • Monetization: ad revenue, fast pass, brand deals.
  • Delivery: full season pitch + backlog.

Webtoon Canvas

Self-publish to build audience momentum and qualify for premium programs.

  • Upload schedule: 1-2x per week.
  • Marketing: banner swaps, creator events.
  • Focus: hook in first 3 panels.

Tapas

Great for bite-sized episodes with tipping and community engagement.

  • Episode length: 15-25 panels.
  • Monetization: Ink, premium unlocks.
  • Bonus: writer/artist collabs.

Kindle Vella

Serial fiction platform for rapid releases and token-based earnings.

  • Episode length: 600-5,000 words.
  • Monetization: reader tokens.
  • Best for: cliffhangers + daily drops.

Radish

Romance and genre fiction powerhouse with paid episode unlocks.

  • Ideal cadence: 3-5x per week.
  • Monetization: paid unlocks + VIP.
  • Needs: strong episodic hooks.

Print & Merch

Bundle your series into print, art books, or collector boxes.

  • Format: 6x9 or 8x10 trim sizes.
  • Distribution: Shopify, Gumroad, IngramSpark.
  • Upsell: signed editions + extras.

Pre-launch checklist

  • Finalize cover art, title, and series logline.
  • Prepare 3-5 episodes for launch week.
  • Write creator bio and tag list for discoverability.
  • Set up socials, newsletter, and creator Q&A.

Release cadence

  • Soft launch: 1 episode + teaser trailer.
  • Launch week: 2-3 episodes for momentum.
  • Ongoing: weekly or twice-weekly drops.
  • Season break: announce early with cliffhanger.

Rights & exclusivity

  • Check platform exclusivity requirements.
  • Retain print/audio rights if possible.
  • Log contract timelines and renewal dates.
  • Document collaborators and royalty splits.

Publishing flow

1. Package the story

Export in vertical scroll, EPUB, or PDF depending on platform requirements.

2. Set monetization

Choose ad share, fast pass, tipping, or paid unlock pricing tiers.

3. Launch and track

Monitor retention, comments, and read-through to refine release cadence.
