The Ashen Gate

The city shimmered beneath a veil of embers, and Lira could hear the bells of the harbor echoing through the smoke. She turned the sigil in her palm, remembering the promise she made to the council: return with answers, not more mysteries.

She crossed the bridge into the Ashen Gate where merchants whispered of a hidden chapter, sealed behind gold and trust. If she unlocked it, the paywall would fall for everyone who believed in her story.

"Stories are gates. Some are locked by fear, others by coin. But every gate has a true name." The words followed her beneath the hanging lanterns as she stepped deeper into the market. Every stallkeeper traded in fragments: a line of prophecy, a memory wrapped in wax, a map drawn in ash.

Behind her, the flare of the phoenix mark pulsed. It was time to decide whether she was an author of endings or beginnings.

Lira pressed onward until the stone avenue opened into a chamber where the gate itself waited, engraved with names from forgotten chapters. She placed her hand on the center sigil, and the letters began to glow one by one like rising embers.

The lock cracked. Not with force, but with recognition. The gate read her story back to her, then lifted. Beyond it, pages stretched in a long vertical corridor, each one waiting to be read, one after another, without interruption.
